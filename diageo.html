The Story in Plain English Think of It Like This

Imagine you own a very famous, very old pub chain. Your pubs serve the best whisky, the best gin, the best beer in the world. For 20 years, business was booming people paid more and more for premium drinks, profits grew, and the share price kept rising.

Then three things happened at once: your biggest customers (Americans) started watching their wallets, your second biggest market (China) stopped bouncing back from COVID, and you had borrowed a lot of money during the good times to expand. Now the interest bills are piling up while sales are shrinking.

Diageo is a Ferrari stuck in traffic. The car is still a Ferrari world-class brands, 180 countries, 200 years of heritage. But right now it is not moving fast, debt is high, and the driver just changed. Our job: buy it cheap before the traffic clears.

The Numbers: Revenue & EBITDA Trend

Revenue peaked at $20.6bn in FY2023 and has been sliding since. EBITDA dropped from $6.5bn to an estimated $5.5bn that's nearly $1bn of profit wiped out in two years.

Why Is Diageo Struggling? The 6 Reasons

Key Headwinds Ranked

Six major forces are dragging on Diageo's performance simultaneously from the tapped-out American consumer to a ballooning debt pile that is now $21.9bn. Each is rated by severity below.

  1. 01
    The American Consumer is Tapped Out (9/10) The US is 38% of all revenue. Americans are cutting back on expensive spirits. Tequila crashed 23% in H1 FY2026. People are trading down from a $60 Don Julio to cheaper options.
  2. 02
    China is Not Recovering (7/10) Asia Pacific fell 12.8% organically. China's post-COVID bounce never fully arrived high youth unemployment, collapsed property wealth, and consumers simply not splurging on premium alcohol.
  3. 03
    Massive Debt Burden (8/10) Net debt has ballooned from $11bn in FY2021 to $21.9bn today. As global interest rates rose sharply from 2022-2024, the cost of servicing that debt became far more expensive.
  4. 04
    FX / Currency Headwinds (5/10) Diageo reports in USD but earns in dozens of currencies. The FX effect knocked hundreds of millions off reported revenues every year entirely outside management's control.
  5. 05
    US Distribution Destocking (6/10) American distributors over-ordered during COVID and have spent two years selling existing stock rather than ordering fresh product from Diageo.
  6. 06
    Premiumisation Strategy Hit a Wall (7/10) For a decade, Diageo's strategy was convincing people to trade UP to premium drinks. With global inflation running hot 2022-2025, people stopped trading up.

A 66% Peak-to-Trough Decline

DGE has fallen from 4,100p (all-time high, Jan 2022) to 1,394p today. Our 12-month target of 1,900p represents 36% upside from here.

My Recommendation The Analyst's Call

  • Buy the Cheapest Guy in the Room: At 11.4x earnings, DGE hasn't been this cheap since 2010. You are buying Johnnie Walker, Guinness and Don Julio at a discount-store price.
  • The New Boss Knows How to Fix Things: Sir Dave Lewis fixed Tesco when it was nearly bankrupt he cut costs, repaired the balance sheet and rebuilt confidence.
  • The Dividend Cut is Actually Good News: By halving the dividend, the company can now use that cash to pay down its $21.9bn debt pile faster.
  • Guinness is a Star: Eight consecutive halves of double-digit growth. Beer is keeping the lights on while whisky and tequila recover.
  • 36% Upside is Hard to Ignore: Current price 1,394p. Our target 1,900p. Morningstar says 2,140p. Even if they are half right, that is still a strong return.
